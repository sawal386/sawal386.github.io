Sawal Acharya

About: I am a first-year master's student in the Institute for Computational and Mathematical Engineering (ICME) at Stanford. Previously, I was a Research Associate at Gibson Lab of Brigham and Women's Hospital. And before that, I studied math and economics at Columbia.

Outside of studies, I enjoy hiking in the mountains, running, biking, and playing & watching football (soccer).

Research Interest: My research interests lie in blending machine learning, statistics, and optimization to make data-driven models more reliable and robust. I am particularly excited about quantifying uncertainties in such models. Before delving into the world of machine learning and statistics, I conducted research in economics.
